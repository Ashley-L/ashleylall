shltr

shltr was produced for my year-end project in my third year at GBC (Sept 2020 - Apr 2021). The goal of this project was to design a part of a city of experiences that did not yet exist.

Team: Ashley Lall, Ayden Kirke, Fatemeh Gharahgozlou, and Mehrnoush Mohammadi

Context

The major insight that guided our work was the idea that transportation can be used to foster connection between people in the city, beyond just getting people from place to place. With this in mind, we decided to tap into how we could alleviate the loneliness experienced at a bus stop.

We decided to develop the bus shelter in the image of a public diary, a hidden bulletin board representing commuters' thoughts and feelings that could be viewed in augmented reality. The idea was that a lonely person at a bus stop could recognize themselves in these notes and realize that even if they are physically alone, they are still a part of a broader community in their city.

Process

Outcome

The final component of this project was to create a working prototype in Unity. I was in charge of developing the app, and as a beginner in Unity, it's safe to say that I learned about image tracking and AR Foundation more broadly.
